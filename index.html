About This Reference

This document series sits above standard chiropractic and physiotherapy undergraduate and CPD-level education. It assumes a competent MSK clinician foundation and builds well beyond it — functioning as a bridge between everyday clinical practice and genuine pain neurology specialisation.

Each section covers: core neurobiological mechanism, clinical presentation, assessment approach, treatment and management considerations, referral flags, and pointers for further specialisation. The content level reflects actual neuroscience — dorsal horn, thalamus, cortex, glial cells, autonomic nervous system — with the mechanistic depth that sharpens clinical reasoning because the underlying biology is understood, not just the surface pattern.

Recommended reading order: Part 1 establishes all foundational mechanisms. Parts 2 and 3 build on them and reference them throughout — start with Part 1 if you haven't already.
